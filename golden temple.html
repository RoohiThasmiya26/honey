GOLDEN Temple

Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is a significant religious and cultural site for Sikhs. Here are some key points about the Golden Temple: Location: City: Amritsar State: Punjab Country: India History: Foundation: The construction of the Golden Temple began in 1581 and was completed in 1604. Founder: Guru Arjan, the fifth Sikh Guru, is credited with planning and overseeing the construction of the temple. Architect: The temple was designed by Guru Arjan. Structure: Architecture: The Golden Temple showcases a blend of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles. Materials: The upper floors of the temple are covered with gold, giving it its iconic appearance. Amrit Sarovar: The temple is surrounded by a large man-made pool (sarovar) of water, which is considered sacred by Sikhs. Religious Significance: Scripture: The Guru Granth Sahib, the central religious scripture of Sikhism, is housed in the temple. Worship: Sikhs visit the temple for worship and reflection. It is a place for prayer, meditation, and community service. Langar: The Golden Temple operates a large community kitchen (langar) that serves free meals to all visitors, regardless of religion, caste, or nationality. Cultural Importance: Symbol: The Golden Temple is a symbol of human brotherhood and equality. It stands as a testament to the Sikh principles of humility and service. Pilgrimage: It is a major pilgrimage site for Sikhs and attracts millions of visitors annually from around the world. Notable Events: Operation Blue Star: In 1984, the Indian Army conducted Operation Blue Star to remove Sikh militants from the temple complex. This operation led to significant damage to the temple and was a highly controversial and traumatic event in Sikh history. Visitation: Open to All: The temple is open to people of all faiths and backgrounds. Dress Code: Visitors are required to cover their heads and remove their shoes before entering the temple premises.
